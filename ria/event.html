Edinburgh

The May edition of Runner's World magazine carried out extensive research into which of the 10 most popular marathons for UK runners were the fastest. A single comparison (like average time) could skew the results so Runner's World used 200 different comparisons to establish which was the fastest marathon and the Edinburgh Marathon came out as the fastest marathon in the UK. Dundee

The route is a mixture of different terrains and has been described as rough and ready and unlike any other marathon in Scotland.

The race begins at Camperdown Park. The route then follows a clockwise course taking in the city's popular Green Circular cycleway. Runners pass through wooded areas, parkland and along a lengthy stretch the northern banks of the River Tay, over a mix of differing terrains, ending up at the finish line back at Camperdown Park.

The route, although not designed traditional competitive clock-watchers will make for an adventurous day out. With relay changeover points (at 8.5 and 17 miles), potential traffic-crossing hotspots and testing undulations it's unlikely you will finish with a personal best. However runners will enjoy any stops, providing an excuse for a momentary breather and chance to admire the fantastic view. Continue reading
